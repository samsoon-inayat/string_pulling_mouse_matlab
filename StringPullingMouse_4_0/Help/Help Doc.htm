Select Zoom: for viewing convenience in the frames display window (FDW); draw a rectangle around the region of interest in the frame.

 

Measure: once a scale has been set with the ※Set Scale§ button, any object or feature in a frame may be measured in the same real units used to set scale, such as cm. The measurement/result is displayed in the MATLAB Command Window.

 

Measure String Thickness: used for other functionalities; simply drag cursor across string to record string thickness.

 

Rows/Cols: for viewing convenience in the FDW.

 

Set Scale: for translation of pixel measurements into real measurements (e.g., cm or mm); drag cursor along an object or feature of known measure (in unit of interest, such as cm) in the frame.

 

Tags: for selection of the features whose tags you would like to view in the FDW.

 

Display Areas: when selected, displays areas in the FDW as opposed to localized dots for ears, nose, and hands (one of these body parts must be selected under Step 4 每 Process).

 

Display Masks: when selected, displays masks in the FDW for the body part selected under Step 4 每 Process.

 

Display Object-Masks: when selected, displays computed masks/objects in the FDW for the body part selected under Step 4 每 Process.

Note: ※areas§ is simply ※computed masks/objects§ without fill (only perimeter line is displayed)

 

Show Masks: in a separate window, displays masks of all body parts in selected frame.

 

Step 1 每 Select Epochs: under ※Start,§ enter the frame number corresponding to the beginning of a string-pulling bout/event. Under ※Stop§ enter the frame number corresponding to the end of the string-pulling bout/event.

You may specify multiple epochs (start/stop pairs) by clicking ※Add row.§ You may erase an epoch by clicking ※Delete row.§

Clicking on any number in the epoch table will take you to that frame in the FDW.

 

Step 2 每 Select Colors: first, ensure that ※Epoch§ is selected under Step 4 每 Process under the heading ※Frames.§

Once that is done, click on each of the five body parts/objects to select the color range (CR) that applies to each. Two new windows will appear, one showing a frame in your epoch and the other displaying several options.

Use ※Add colors§ to add colors to the CR applying to the body part/object selected previously (e.g., String).

To increase or decrease the number of color clusters (subdivisions) a selected region is divided into (when using ※Add colors§), use ※Edit Number of Clusters (n),§ where n represent the current number of color clusters/subdivisions.

Use ※Remove colors§ to remove colors to the CR applying to the body part/object selected previously (e.g., String). That is, ※Add colors§ and ※Remove colors§ are exactly opposite in function.

Use ※Save colors§ periodically to ensure that the CR selected is being saved.

Use ※Next frame§ to move on to another frame in the epoch once you are satisfied with your selections in the current frame.

Use ※Skip frame§ to skip color selection in the current frame.

Use ※Undo§ to undo a recent addition or removal of colors to the CR.

Use ※Exit§ to exit both color selection windows.

Once you have completed specifying the CR for a body parts/object, move on to the next, until a CR has been specified for all five body parts/objects.

 

Step 3 每 Find Zoom Window for Masks: use ※Find Body Box§ to find the smallest box (area) that contains the animal＊s body. To modify the automatically selected body box, you may click ※Change§ to manually enter coordinates, or you may alter the margins using the inputs on the right side. When using ※Change,§ note that the first pair of numbers represents the x and y coordinates of the top left corner of the body box and that the second pair of numbers represents the bottom right corner of the body box; the origin is the top left corner of the frame. For example, in order to increase the width of the body box, one would decrease the first number and/or increase the third number. If margins are altered and are to your satisfaction, ensure that the ※Overwrite§ box is checked prior to clicking ※Find Body Box§ again.

To find head boxes, first use ※Test§ to view the head box for a sample frame. Based on this, alter the margin inputs on the right (under ※Head,§ not ※Body§) and retest accordingly until the animal＊s entire head is contained in the head box. Then, use ※Find Head Boxes§ to find the head boxes through the epoch.

 

Step 4 每 Process: under the ※Objects§ heading select one of the five body parts/objects (in the order they are shown, beginning with String), and under the ※Frames§ heading select the frame(s) that you would like to process (find masks for, etc.).

Note: the functionality of the ※Find Objects§ subpanel is dependent on processing that must be completed under the ※Mask Processing§ subpanel first. However, the functionality of the ※Gross Motion Assessment§ subpanel is independent of both the ※Mask Processing§ and ※Find Objects§ subpanels.

Under ※Mask Processing,§ select the processing method (KNN, Range Search, or Grid Way) you would like to employ and click ※Find Mask§ to find masks using the selected method. Experiment with the three available methods, as one may be best suited to your data set. If Range Search is selected as the processing method, then you may specify a ※Radius§ under ※Specs.§ This radius is the Euclidian distance from a color in 3-D space (the three dimensions being RGB); the resulting sphere is the color range that the program will identify as the body part/object being processed; such a sphere is delineated around every original color selected for the body part/object in previous steps.

Use ※Erase Mask§ to erase masks from the frame(s) specified under ※Frames.§

Note: masks should be found for parts in the order shown (string first, then body, then ears, etc.)

Once masks have been found, use ※Find Object§ to find computed masks/objects for the frame(s). When using ※Find Object§ on hands, hands in the first frame must be selected manually. Furthermore, it is advisable to stop processing immediately after an incorrect label to prevent errors from propagating through the remainder of frames and affecting accuracy.

To correct an error, use ※Find Manually.§ You may select ※Draw Region§ prior to using ※Find Manually§ if you wish to manually draw a polygon around the body part/object of interest. If ※Draw Region§ is not selected, then you may simply draw a rectangle around (containing) the body part/object of interest, following which the program will identify the body part/object of interest using masks. When processing hands, you may alternatively select ※Rectangle§ prior to using ※Find Manually;§ this allows the drawing of a smaller rectangle for each hand such that each rectangles contains not a whole hand, but rather a subsection of a hand, which will define the location (coordinates) of the hand in the frame in question.

Use ※Erase Object§ to erase computed masks/objects from the frame(s) specified under ※Frames.§

Use ※Descriptive Stats§ under ※Find Objects§ to view descriptive statistics based on computed masks/objects.

Under ※Gross Motion Assessment,§ use ※Estimate Motion§ to perform optical flow analysis on the frame(s) specified under ※Frames.§ Use ※Descriptive Statistics§ to view descriptive statistics based on masks. Use ※Find Entropy§ to quantify randomness (on the basis of color) along the temporal dimension (across the selected frame(s)) for every pixel. Use ※Find PCs§ to perform principle component analysis. Use ※Find ICs§ to perform independent component analysis.

For the processes available under the ※Gross Motion Assessment§ and ※Mask Processing§ subpanels, computational/processing time can be reduced by enabling the ※Reduce Image Size§ option and specifying an image reduction factor; an image reduction factor of 4 is suggested as a good starting point. Lower image reduction factors yield finer analysis but increase processing time as the trade-off. The lowest image reduction factor (i.e., that which produces the finest analysis and requires the greatest processing time) is 1. The image ※Reduce Image Size§ feature does not apply to finding objects.

Note: ※Overwrite§ must be selected whenever you wish to redo/reprocess something under Step 4 每 Process.

 

Body, Smallest area to neglect: when finding computed masks/objects, high-density areas of the mask are identified and clustered together. The value entered here is used as follows: if a cluster (as defined in the previous sentence) is equal to or smaller in size/area/number of constituent pixels than the value entered here, then it is discarded and will not be used to compute the computed mask/object.
